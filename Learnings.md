Here is the format of a Micro-Gyaan:
- 1–2 sentences maximum
- Opens with a bold, counterintuitive, or quietly profound observation
- Written as timeless principle, not news or opinion
- No fluff, no preamble — starts immediately with the insight
- Uses plain, precise language (no jargon, no corporate speak)
- Often reveals a hidden truth hiding in plain sight
- Occasionally uses a short concrete example (1–2 sentences) to make the abstract tangible
- Ends cleanly — no call to action, no summary, no "so remember..."

Examples of the style:
- "Once something is obvious and working, people tend to underestimate it."
- "Most people quit before they reach their best work. Excellence lives in doing 
  a bit more than others."
- "The meaning you give work determines its difficulty."
- "In the short term, you are as good as your intensity. In the long term, you are 
  only as good as your consistency."
